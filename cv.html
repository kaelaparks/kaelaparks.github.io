Professional Work

I am a Disability Services professional who has been actively engaged at the institutional, regional, and national levels. I serve in leadership positions within professional associations and have been fortunate to have experience working on both grant funded universal design projects and collaborative awareness building efforts. The annotated links below hilight some of the projects and initiatives I have worked on.

Collaborations

  • Dis/Representation is a disability studies community event offered collaboratively through Portland Community College, Portland State University, and the Disability Arts and Culture Project.
  • AHEAD Access to Technology Series As co-chair of the Standing Committee on Technology I have been responsible for facilitating the cultivation and deployment of a webinar series as well as a conference strand.
  • Reel Eyes Showcasae of Films By and About People with Disabilites is an event that I facilitated for several years. Films were shown at multiple locations across the state of Alaska. It was a wonderful experience and I was able to design and deliver courses that ran alongside the series. Different years the course was offered through different colleges, so there were a variety of disciplines participating. Courses were offered in Psychology, Education, Social Work, and Guidance cross-listed across levels.
  • State of Alaska Web Accessibility Initiative was a partnership betweeen the University of Alaska and the State of Alaska. I worked closely with the State ADA Coordinator to plan and deliver two day trainings twice per year. We brought web developers and content authors from the state and from the university and held joint trainings that were capped at 30. We had full crowds each time we offered it, with some repeat customers. The trainings were offered from 2008 to 2012 with a final session offered jointly with Portland Community College in 2013.
  • ADA Celebrations in Alaska were a very collaborative undertaking with both financial contributions on the part of the University, which took advocacy to secure, and investments of time and energy.
  • Disability History Exhibit chronicles the treatment of people with disabilities over time, from multiple angles including a moral, medical, and social perspective. I reached out to the group who had created it and brokered a deal. I offered to create an accessible electronic version in exchange for half off the cost of the display.
